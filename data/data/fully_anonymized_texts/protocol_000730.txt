XII. STUDY DESIGN/METHODOLOGY  
 
 
 
This will be a single institution, prospective, blinded, randomized, controlled 
effectiveness study with 2-armed parallel-group sequential design to assess the efficacy 
of liposomal bupi[INVESTIGATOR_124176]. Exparel® liposomal bupi[INVESTIGATOR_124177]-approved suspension 
consisting of a liposomal form of the local anesthetic bupi[INVESTIGATOR_10319]; it is to be injected 
intraoperatively in the surgical wound just prior to closing the incision.

the two study groups, i.e., liposomal bupi[INVESTIGATOR_124178]-liposomal bupi[INVESTIGATOR_124179]. Patients in both groups will receive the standard 
opi[INVESTIGATOR_124180]. Patients will receive a single dose 
of intravenous acetaminophen perioperatively as per the standard practice by [CONTACT_124204].

holding area, the inpatient wards, or in the outpatient clinic, patients will be educated on

XVII.  DATA SAFETY MON ITORING  
 
 
In view of the uncertainty attached to the treatment effects in this heterogeneous 
population, owing to limited currently available data, an independent Data Safety 
Monitoring Comm ittee, will oversee the progress and: 
 
i. Monitor avoidable risks associated with patient data and confidentiality. 
 
 
 
ii. Monitor achievement of trial recruitme nt targets, appropriateness of 
trial size, 
 
iii. Evaluate interim analysis and advise reme dial actions in case of 
superiority/futility situations 
 
Two interim analyses will be conducted after the enrollment of the 94th and 188th patient, 
respectively using a group-sequential design. During these interim assessments the 
DSMC will decide on need for continuation based on the evidence that a reliable 
conclusion can be drawn based on available data (superiority) or unlikely to observe a 
statistical difference (futility). As for instance, if one between-group difference is 
demonstrably inferior to the other using a three-stage group-sequential overall nominal 
value of 0.05 14 (alpha spending of 0.0002, 0.012 and .0462 at 94, 188 and 330 
participants accrued, respectively ) the study will be stopped early and the results will be 
reported to the IRB and published.
