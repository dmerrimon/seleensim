Participants will attend weekly visits while semaglutide dosage is increased to 1.0mg over a period of 10 weeks. At scheduled intervals, participants will attend 2 laboratory sessions involving a validated smoking lapse procedure to characterize medication effects on nicotine reinstatement and laboratory self-administration.   Objectives:  Primary Objective: To examine effects of semaglutide vs. placebo on nicotine reinstatement and self-administration under laboratory conditions.    Secondary Objectives: To examine medication vs. placebo group differences in 1) changes in naturalistic cigarette consumption, and 2) changes in body weight over the course of the treatment period.   Tertiary Objectives: To generate preliminary data on medication effects on laboratory responses to nicotine, and cue-induced craving among regular smokers during treatment with semaglutide.

By [CONTACT_117413]-validation of both preclinical and Phase III results, human experimental studies serve a cornerstone role in translational drug development [7, 21, 22, 29]. An ancillary advantage of these studies is the ability to test putative mechanisms of pharmacotherapy effects under controlled experimental conditions [26]. Importantly, recent reviews have also highlighted areas in which laboratory studies can be improved. First, many laboratory studies are limited by a sole focus on self-report outcomes (e.g., craving), which lack a direct analogue in animal models [30, 31]. Relative to self-report outcomes, drug self-administration outcomes appear more sensitive to pharmacotherapy effects, and may improve consistency in findings across preclinical and human studies [21]. Additionally, self-reported craving - while undoubtedly a clinically important construct - is neither necessary nor sufficient for self-administration, supporting the need to prioritize objective self-administration outcomes [32]. Another limitation of most human lab studies concerns their discordance with Phase III trials in terms of sample characteristics. A key limitation, as highlighted in a recent systematic review, is that human laboratory studies of smoking pharmacotherapy typi[INVESTIGATOR_117387]-motivated smokers, limiting comparisons with Phase III trials [29]. Importantly, evidence shows that recruiting non-motivated smokers undermines the ability to detect medication effects, whereas recruiting smokers with some motivation to quit (even if not engaged in an active quit attempt) improves effect sizes in medication screening trials [24].  2.3 RISK/BENEFIT ASSESSMENT    2.3.1 KNOWN POTENTIAL RISKS  Semaglutide (Ozempic®) received FDA approval in [ADDRESS_130939] to diet and exercise to achieve glycemic control in adults with type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2D). The simulated smoking lapse procedure was developed by [CONTACT_117419], who is a consultant on this project [23, 52]. The McKee paradigm is the only validated laboratory procedure that models smoking reinstatement and self-administration in the same procedure [23]. In this procedure, a nicotine deprivation period (e.g., overnight abstinence) provides a context to study medication effects on reinstatement [23]. Given the requirement of overnight abstinence prior to the lapse session, it is important to maximize the likelihood of the participant adhering to these instructions. Failure to maintain overnight abstinence could result in having to reschedule the session. Therefore, participants will be offered a bonus of $25 for completing the overnight abstinence requirement, as additional incentive to adhere to the abstinence instructions. Sessions will be conducted in a laboratory space equipped with a fume hood; participants will be seated near the hood and will be instructed to direct exhaled smoke into the hood. Research staff will be required to don protective equipment (e.g., N95 mask and face shield) when present in the room during the time of the task, and will remain outside the room during the procedure, to the extent possible.  Upon beginning the task, participants will be presented with a package of their preferred cigarettes (participants will be asked to bring one unopened pack in to the session). Participants will be given the choice to initiate cigarette smoking, or to delay smoking for up to 50 minutes in exchange for monetary reinforcement. Prior studies show that a reinforcement schedule of $1 for every 5 minutes results in participants resisting, on average, for 25 minutes, providing ideal variability in time to reinstatement [23]. If no lapse occurs (i.e., the participant resists for 50 minutes), participants will not be forced to smoke a cigarette. Following the initial decision to smoke, participants will complete assessments of craving, withdrawal, and subjective effects (described below).

This timeframe also allows sufficient time to reach steady-state plasma concentration levels (achieved within 4-5 weeks of initiating the starting dose). Secondary, sensitivity analyses will be conducted to test the robustness of study findings for violations of the assumption that data is missing at random. Specifically, we will examine if baseline variables are associated with data missingness (for instance, age, sex, level of substance use) using t-tests and chi-square analyses (for continuous and dichotomous variables, respectively). Baseline variables that are associated with data missingness will be included as covariates in additional adjusted models (that adjust for the baseline variables related to missingness). Assumptions for the statistical methods being used will be tested. Should there be evidence of violations of these assumptions (e.g., presence of outlying and influential cases, distributional concerns for residuals, and so on), appropriate corrective actions will be taken.
